## Resumo em 3 bullets
- **Topico 1**: HDMI ports vary in capabilities; using the wrong one can limit Chromecast 4K resolution, HDR, and refresh rates.
- **Topico 2**: HDMI 2.1 ports offer up to 48 Gbps bandwidth versus 18 Gbps for HDMI 2.0, enabling better 4K/120Hz performance.
- **Topico 3**: ARC/eARC ports are optimized for audio; use them for soundbars, not for streaming devices unless no HDMI 2.1 is available.

## Por que isso importa para devs
Selecting the correct HDMI port is critical for developers and tech teams deploying media devices in production environments. Incorrect port usage can silently degrade streaming quality, cause compatibility issues, and increase support tickets. Understanding HDMI version differences and port labeling ensures optimal device performance and user experience.

## O que muda na pratica
- Audit all deployed Chromecast and streaming devices to confirm they are connected to HDMI 2.1 ports labeled "4K," "120Hz," or similar.
- Update deployment documentation and checklists to specify HDMI port requirements for 4K/HDR-capable devices.
- Train support staff to verify HDMI port type and signal resolution via TV settings before escalating hardware issues.

## Contexto rapido
- HDMI 2.1 supports up to 48 Gbps bandwidth, enabling 4K at 120Hz and advanced HDR; HDMI 2.0 supports up to 18 Gbps.
- ARC/eARC ports are designed for audio return channel; using them for streaming devices may limit video performance if HDMI 2.1 is available.
