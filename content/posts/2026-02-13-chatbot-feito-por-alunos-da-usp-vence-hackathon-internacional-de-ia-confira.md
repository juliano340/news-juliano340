## Resumo em 3 bullets
- **Topico 1**: USP students built a WhatsApp-based AI fact-checking chatbot that won an international hackathon.
- **Topico 2**: The bot, named "Tá certo isso AI?", verifies text, images, audio, video, and stickers in real time.
- **Topico 3**: A public analytics dashboard tracks 893+ checks and shows false content percentages.

## Por que isso importa para devs
This project demonstrates how lightweight, platform-native AI tools can be deployed rapidly to combat misinformation at scale. By embedding verification directly into WhatsApp—a channel used by billions—the solution removes friction for non-technical users while maintaining transparency through public analytics. Its hackathon origin and international recognition also highlight the potential for academic-led innovation to address pressing societal challenges without requiring massive infrastructure investment.

## O que muda na pratica
- Integrate WhatsApp Business API or third-party chatbot frameworks to prototype similar verification workflows.
- Build a lightweight analytics pipeline to aggregate and visualize user-submitted content classifications in real time.
- Design a multi-modal input pipeline capable of processing text, images, audio, and video for automated fact-checking.

## Contexto rapido
- Developed in 10 hours during a RAIA hackathon focused on fake news solutions at USP.
- Presented at the Brazil Conference at Harvard and MIT after winning the AI4Good challenge.
- Operates via a single WhatsApp number (35 8424-8271) and supports group mentions for inline checks.
